Vegan Benedict

The Thing about it is...

There is nothing as good as a real Eggs Benedict!

But, for our vegan and fasting Orthodox friends, we need to provide a nice substitute for them so they can enjoy the very things we all like!


The Inners of this Thingy:

Your are going to need the following items to make this magical thing;

  • 1 Block (225g) Extra Firm Smoked Tofu, pressed to remove water, I used (tofoo brand)
  • 2 tbs Cooking Oil, for frying
  • Pinch Black Salt (optional for an "eggy" flavour, if not use sea salt)
  • Pinch Ground Pepper
    • Hollandaise Sauce
  • 1/2 cup/120ml Soy Milk (this recipe will only work with soy milk)
  • 3 tbs White Wine Vinegar
  • 1/2 cup/120ml Yellow Rapeseed Oil
  • 1/2 tsp English Mustard
  • Pinch Sea Salt & Ground Pepper
    • Sautéed Spinach
  • 4 Big Handfuls of Baby Spinach & Pinch Seasoning
    • Serve with:
  • 2 toasted breakfast muffins, small bunch of fresh chives, chopped fine, pinch of cress, a handful of cherry tomatoes halved

The Method:

This is how we are going to put the whole damn thing together;

  1. First up make the Hollandaise, in a measuring jug add the soy milk, vinegar, seasoning & mustard. Using an electric hand/stick blender, blend until mixed well.
  2. Whilst blending slowly trickle in the oil until the sauce starts to thicken up. Once you’ve added all the oil it should be thick & creamy, but still pourable.
  3. If your sauce is too thick stir in a few additional tablespoons of soy milk. Check for seasoning.
  4. Cover the sauce with cling film & set aside until you’re ready to serve.
  5. Cut the tofu into rectangles, around 4mm thick. Pat dry with kitchen paper then preheat a non-stick frying pan over a medium heat.
  6. Add the oil to the pan, followed by the tofu. Pan fry until golden on each side, this should take 3-4 minutes. Season with the black salt if using & pepper.
  7. Remove the tofu from the pan and set aside, turn the heat up high a touch more oil & when it starts to smoke & all the spinach (don’t worry it will wilt down quickly).
  8. Cook the spinach for 1 minute, stirring quickly. Season with a pinch of salt & pepper then removes from the heat.
  9. The spinach will contain lots of water so I always press it with a clean kitchen towel to soak up excess liquid.
  10. Serve everything on top of toasted bagels, then sprinkle over some chopped chives. Serve immediately.
